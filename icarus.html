Fly Too Close to the Sun

Fri Sept 29 2023

The myth of Icarus is one of the oldest and most famous Greek myths. It has been told and retold for centuries and has been featured in countless works of art, literature, and music.

The story of Icarus

Icarus was the son of Daedalus, a skilled craftsman and inventor. He built two pairs of wings out of feathers and wax. He warned Icarus not to fly too close to the sun or too close to the sea but to follow his flight path.

The possible reasons why Icarus decided to fly higher and higher.

What did Icarus hope to achieve by flying higher?

  • Hubris

    This is something foolish, and I don't think it was right at all.

  • Ambition

    Icarus may have been ambitious to the point of recklessness. He may have wanted to fly higher than anyone else had ever flown before, even if it meant putting himself in danger.

  • Curiosity

    Icarus may have been simply curious about what it would be like to fly as close to the sun as possible. He may have wanted to see the world from a different perspective and experience the thrill of flying higher than anyone else had ever dared to fly.

  • Youthful exuberance

    Icarus was a young man, and young people are often impulsive and reckless. He may have been so excited about the experience of flying that he forgot about the dangers involved.

  • Temptation

    The sun is a powerful and alluring symbol. It is the source of life and light, and it can be very tempting to get closer to it. Icarus may have been tempted by the sun's beauty and power, even though he knew it was dangerous and fatal.

What the story tells us

We are all mortal and have limitations in our knowledge and understanding:

No matter how much we expand our knowledge, there will always be areas where our understanding falls short. However, anyone attempting to challenge these limits will face consequences for their actions.

My perspective diverges from the traditional morals of the story:

Consider this

Did Icarus not realize that his fervent desire to ascend to the Sun would ultimately lead him towards his demise? Yet, Icarus chose to soar to great heights. We all understand that our days on this earth are limited, but opting to embrace that finite existence while striving for the extraordinary, against all odds, is what makes Icarus my hero.

  • My viewpoint can be succinctly captured by a single question: "If Icarus hadn't harbored such ambition, if Icarus hadn't dared to shatter the confines of convention, would we be discussing Icarus's legacy today?"
  • To me, Icarus epitomizes not recklessness but valor and ambition. Icarus's enduring legacy had granted Icarus immortality, ensuring that every time someone recounts the tale of Icarus, Icarus becomes alive.
